        <div class="highlight-blue">
          <h3>What is melanoma?</h3>
          <p>Melanoma is a very serious skin cancer characterized by the uncontrolled growth of cells that produce pigment, the substance in skin that produces color. Melanomas may appear suddenly and without warning. They are found most frequently on the face and neck, upper back and legs, but can occur anywhere on the body.</p>
        </div>
        <div>
          <span class="italic">Regular skin self-exams may help detect skin cancer <span class="bold">early</span>—when it’s smaller and easier to treat. Become familiar with the way your skin and moles look. The first symptom of melanoma is often a change in a mole or the appearance of a new mole. The ABCDE rule can help you tell a normal mole from one that might be cancerous.</span> 
        </div>
      </section>

              <p class="rule_description">Melanomas are frequently asymmetrical: the shape of one half does not match the other.</p>

          </div>
          <div>
            <p class="rule_title"><span class="bold">B</span> = Border</p>
            <div class="image_title">
            <img src="https://images.ctfassets.net/1ny4yoiyrqia/1w7j9kf6RFzUDZNaOPNQse/fb227289107bb54105abd5e111b3457a/B-border.png">
            <p class="rule_description">Melanomas frequently have uneven or irregular borders (ragged or notched edges).</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p class="rule_title"><span class="bold">C</span> = Color</p>
            <div class="image_title">

            <p class="rule_description">Melanomas often contain multiple shades of brown or black but can sometimes be mixed with white, gray, blue, or red. Some melanomas will show a loss of color in a preexisting mole or in the area surrounding the mole.</p>
          </div>
          </div>
          <div>
            <p class="rule_title"><span class="bold">D</span> = Diameter</p>

            <p class="rule_description">Melanomas are often larger than 6 mm (1/4 inch) in diameter. However, with increased awareness about early detection, about 30% of melanomas are found when they are less than 6 mm in diameter.</p>
